Extended Presidential Election Analysis 2025

This interactive dashboard presents a comprehensive analysis of the 2025 Presidential Election in Romania. The analysis includes national results, urban vs. rural voting patterns, regional variations, turnout statistics, major city analyses, and detailed county-level support for all candidates. Use the tabs below to explore different aspects of the election results.

National Election Results

This visualization shows the national results for all candidates in the 2025 Presidential Election. The bar chart displays the absolute number of votes received by each candidate, while the pie chart illustrates the percentage of the total vote.

Urban vs. Rural Voting Patterns

This visualization compares voting patterns in urban and rural areas. The bar chart shows the percentage of votes received by each candidate in urban and rural areas, while the green line indicates the gap between urban and rural support (positive values mean higher urban support).

Regional Voting Patterns

This visualization shows how the top three candidates performed across different regions of Romania. The grouped bar chart allows for direct comparison of regional strengths and weaknesses for each candidate.

Major Cities Comparison

This visualization compares how the top candidates performed in major Romanian cities (Iași, Cluj-Napoca, Timișoara, Sibiu, Brașov, Oradea, Craiova, and București). The grouped bar chart shows the percentage of votes received by each candidate in each city.

Turnout in Major Cities

This visualization shows the voter turnout percentage in each major Romanian city. The red dotted line indicates the average turnout across these cities, allowing for easy identification of cities with above or below average participation.

Voter Turnout Analysis

This visualization analyzes voter turnout patterns. The bar chart shows the turnout percentage overall, in urban areas, and in rural areas. The pie chart illustrates the composition of voters and non-voters in urban and rural areas.
